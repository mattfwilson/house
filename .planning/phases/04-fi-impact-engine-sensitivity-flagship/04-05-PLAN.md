<objective>
Close the two coupled tornado-driver gaps that share the schema + sensitivity surface:

1. GAP 1 (BLOCKER / SC5 / ASMP-02): the tornado's `tax` driver perturbs `assumptions.tax.propertyRateAnnual`, a leaf NO FI/TCO calc reads — property tax flows through the town-resolved mill rate (`tco.resolvedMillRate`). The tax driver therefore produces ZERO FI-date swing. Fix: introduce an OPTIONAL, overridable mill-rate at the assumption boundary that `computeTco` honors, and rewire the tax driver to perturb THAT live rate (seeded from the resolved town rate) relatively (×(1±taxBandRelative), L6) so the perturbation actually flows through `annualPropertyTax` in `ownerHousingAt` and `buyMonthlyOutflowAt`. The tornado contract is preserved: one stored band, re-run the SAME pure `fiImpact`, no bespoke per-driver projection math (Pitfall 10).

2. GAP 2 (CRITICAL / CR-01): `divideBySwr` has no zero/negative guard — a zero `swr.rate` crashes via `Money.of("Infinity")`; a negative rate silently yields a negative FI target read as "reached at month 0." Both reachable through the tornado's own `swr` driver low band AND through unguarded user assumptions. Fix: a positivity `.refine` on `swr.rate` at the schema boundary (mirroring the `targetSavingsRate`/`downPaymentPct` [0,1) precedent), a defense-in-depth guard in `divideBySwr`, and a clamp/guard on the tornado's `swr` low-band so a swept band cannot drive SWR <= 0.

Purpose: make the tornado honest — the tax row swings, and no driver can drive the engine into a crash or a false month-0 FI date.
Output: a live, perturbable mill rate; a guarded SWR divide; new tests; the green suite preserved with NO golden regeneration.
</objective>
